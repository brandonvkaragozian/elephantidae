MIXED INTEGER PROGRAM FOR CAMERA PLACEMENT
================================================================================

PROBLEM FORMULATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Decision Variables:
  x[j] ∈ {0,1}  : Camera placed at location j
  y[i] ∈ [0,1]  : Coverage level of cell i

Objective Function:
  maximize: Σ_i (demand[i] * y[i])

Constraints:
  1. Budget: Σ_j x[j] ≤ 16
  2. Coverage: y[i] ≤ Σ_j detectability[i,j] * x[j]  ∀i

COMPONENTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Demand (Importance):
  Definition: Weighted combination of elephant activity metrics
  Components: 50% visit count + 30% trajectory diversity + 20% entry points
  Range: [0, 1] normalized
  Mean: 0.093
  High-demand cells (>0.8): 6

Detectability (Visibility):
  Definition: Probability camera at j can detect elephants at i
  Formula: 1 - occlusion_factor(j → i)
  Occlusion: Line-of-sight interpolation
    Forest: 80% blocking
    Water: 90% blocking
    Settlements: 70% blocking
    Crops: 30% blocking
  Binary threshold: >0.3 (>30% clear line-of-sight)
  Range: [0, 1] continuous, [0,1] binary

SOLVER
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Algorithm: Branch-and-cut (CBC solver)
Time limit: 120 seconds
Status: Optimal
Objective: 99.618
